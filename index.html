Who are we?

We are a Bay Area student-run quartet, rehearsing regularly throughout the year and receiving coaching from Omid Assadi. We have played music together since childhood through programs of the Villa Sinfonia Foundation , a nonprofit music organization. In 2013, we founded the Velvet String Quartet. Our group can be found busking or performing in various gigs and concerts throughout the year. Our repertoire includes music ranging from Mozart quartets, to tangos, to Coldplay.

Raina

Raina Christeson studies violin in the Bay Area with Catherine Van Hoesen, having received much of her violin education from Roy Oakley. She performs regularly as assistant concertmaster of both Ruth Asawa San Francisco School of the Arts Orchestra and Villa Chamber Orchestra. Raina was recently featured at Other Minds Festival and Switchboard Music Festival, serving as concertmaster of Ruth Asawa SOTA Orchestra, the first student group ever invited to perform at either festival. She has also performed at the Junior Bach Festival, and has toured Washington, D.C. and France with Villa Sinfonia Orchestra, performing at the Kennedy Center, and La Madeleine Church in Paris. Raina participates in the chamber music program at Ruth Asawa SOTA, and is a founding member of the Velvet String Quartet. She plans to pursue degrees in Violin Performance and Environmental Studies in college.

Narain

Narain has been studying violin under Lynn Oakley for nine years. He is a member of the Villa Sinfonia Orchestra and Villa Chamber Orchestra and has performed on tours to England, Scotland, Washington D.C., and France with them. In 2014 he had the privilege to participate in the annual Junior Bach festival where he performed the Bach D minor Chaccone at the Old First Church. In addition to his regular private lessons, he has also received instruction from Roy Oakley, Omid Assadi, David Ryther, Nancy Ewing, Ilana Matfis, and Carol Rice at Zephyr Chamber Music Camp which he attends every summer. His experience with these instructors inspired him to pursue music education himself. He has interned at ELM (Enriching Lives Through Music) where he was an assistant music teacher to Karsten Windt at the San Pedro school. He is currently teaching two young violin students on a regular basis. Currenly he is the concertmaster his highschool and is a founding member of the Velvet String Quartet.

Carlyn

Carlyn S has studied cello for over 9 years, and is a founding player for the Velvet String Quartet. Her solo performances include Brahms and Beethoven sonatas, Paganini and Elgar. Carlyn is currently the principal cellist for her high school ensemble as well as a longstanding member of the Villa Sinfonia Orchestra and Chamber Orchestra of San Francisco. With VSO, she has performed in Edinburgh, London, San Francisco and New York. Carlyn studies with Carol Rice, principal cellist of the Berkeley Symphony.

Laila

Laila is 15 years old and has lived in San Francisco all her life. She started playing violin when she was 6 years old. As soon as she switched to viola she never went back. Laila is finishing her sophomore year at Ruth Asawa School of the Arts where she participates in numerous performances. In addition to SOTA and the Velvet String Quartet, Laila plays viola for Villa Sinfonia. Laila is a fluent Spanish speaker and plays guitar in her free time. She is a huge lover of punk and her favorite band is AFI. Recently she has also become a member of the San Francisco Youth Orchestra.
